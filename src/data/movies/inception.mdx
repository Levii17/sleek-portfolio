The thing that keeps me coming back to this one isn't the twist ending,
it's the construction. Every layer of the dream has its own internal logic
and its own pacing, and the film trusts you to track all of it at once
without stopping to explain itself.

## What works

The heist-movie structure underneath the sci-fi concept is what makes it
rewatchable. Strip away the dream layers and it's a classic team-assembles-
for-one-job story, which gives the film a shape even when the premise gets
abstract.

## What I think about differently now

On a rewatch, the emotional throughline (guilt, grief, letting go) does
more of the work than the mechanics of the dream-sharing technology. The
spinning top ending matters less to me now than it did the first time.

## Would I recommend it

Yes, and it's one of the few films where I'd actively recommend watching
it twice.
